Kent

Kent is a city in the U.S. state of Ohio and the largest city in Portage County which is located along Cuyahoga River in Northeast Ohio. Kent had the population 28.904 in the 2010 Census and 32,345 in the 2013 estimate.

Kent was settled in 1805 and was known for many years as Franklin Mills. Settlers were attracted to the area due to its location along the Cuyahoga River as a place for water-powered mills. Later development came in the 1830s and 1840s as a result of the village's position along the route of the Pennsylvania and Ohio Canal. Leading up to the American Civil War, Franklin Mills was noted for its activity in the Underground Railroad. With the decline of the canal and the emergence of the railroad, the village became the home of the Atlantic and Great Western Railroad maintenance shops through the influence of Marvin Kent.
